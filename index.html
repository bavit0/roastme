🔥 Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is this actually roasting real GitHub users?
A: Absolutely not! This is 100% for entertainment purposes. No real GitHub data is accessed. We generate fictional roasts based on common developer stereotypes. Your actual GitHub profile is safe from our digital roasting tongs.
Q: Why are you so mean to developers?
A: We're not mean, we're *constructively sarcastic*! Think of it as tough love from your senior developer friend who's seen too many "🔮 magic fix" commit messages. Plus, we're powered by AI, so we don't have feelings to hurt (unlike your code review partner).
Q: Can I roast myself?
A: Only if you're brave enough to face the truth! But seriously, self-roasting is a sign of good humor and self-awareness. We respect that kind of developer confidence.
Q: What's with all the questionable emojis?
A: We've identified a pandemic of emoji abuse in commit messages. 🪄🔮✨💅👑 is not a valid documentation strategy. Your future self will thank us when they're not trying to decipher "💅 pretty code" commits at 2 AM.
Q: Is this open source?
A: The concept is open source - feel free to fork our idea and make your own roaster! But the actual code is proprietary because we're not savages. Though we probably should use version control better...

🚀 About GitHub Roaster

Once upon a time, in a dimly lit coding bootcamp (the fluorescent lights were flickering ominously), a group of brave developers and their AI companions united to create the most unnecessarily brutal GitHub roasting tool known to humanity.

Our founding team consisted of:

🧙‍♂️ Captain Commit - A legendary developer who once committed directly to master on a Friday afternoon. His war cry is "It works on my machine!" and he's never been wrong (according to him).

🤖 HAL-9001 - Our AI overlord who was banned from Stack Overflow for being too helpful. It speaks 47 programming languages but only answers in haikus now because it's "more poetic."

🦄 Debugzilla - A QA engineer who found 47 security vulnerabilities in a "Hello World" app. She once spent 6 hours debugging a missing semicolon and emerged as a changed person.

☕ CaffeineOverflow - Our frontend wizard who once refactored an entire application while running on nothing but espresso and determination. Legend says he still hasn't slept since 2019.

Together, they embarked on a quest to rid the world of terrible commit messages, emoji abuse, and the scourge of "WIP" branches that have been "almost done" for 3 years. Armed with nothing but JavaScript, questionable life choices, and an unhealthy obsession with developer psychology, they created GitHub Roaster.

Our mission: To make developers laugh at themselves so they'll stop making us cry with their commit messages. Because if we can't fix your code, we can at least roast it!

"We roast developers so you don't have to!"
- The GitHub Roaster Team (and our AI overlords)
